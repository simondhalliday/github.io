Bio

I am an assistant professor of economics at Smith College in Northampton, MA, USA. I work in experimental economics and the microeconomics of development (with a focus on Sub-Saharan Africa). I have strong side interests in reproducible research, data literacy and economics education.

Working papers:

  • Halliday, Simon and Jonathan Lafky, 'Reciprocity through Ratings: An Experiment'. We use a new experimental design to examine behavior of reviewers to see whether they will punish or extort (or encourage bribing by) those they are rating.
  • Halliday, Simon, 'Data Literacy in Economic Development'. I explain a set of exercises building towards students analyzing a cross-sectional dataset (NIDS, 2008 and 2014/15) to understand poverty measurement and analysis of inequality in MS Excel.
